Learning Tips

Your Study Environment

Creating a productive area of study allows you to get the most out of your study time. Effective time management, organized books, a strong motivation and the ability to take notes, are all key tools required for an effective study environment.
What's right for you might not work for someone else. The ideal study environment would be in a designated area with a table and no distractions. This means turning off your phone for the duration of your study to get rid of any distractions. Poor lighting is a big cause of eyestrain and headaches, so the direction and intensitiy of the light in the room are important factors. Your working area should be free of any clutter so materials can be placed in an organized manner which keeps everything in a place it's easy to find.

Studying Checklist

1. Are you in a quiet area?

   
   

2. Is there good lighting in the room?

   
   

3. Is you study area free of clutter?

   
   

4. Are your materials laid out in an organized manner?

   
   

5. Is your phone out of the way?
